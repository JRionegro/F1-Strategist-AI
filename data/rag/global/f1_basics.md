# Formula 1 Basics

## Race Format

### Race Weekend Structure

- **Friday**: Free Practice 1 (FP1) and Free Practice 2 (FP2)
- **Saturday**: Free Practice 3 (FP3) and Qualifying
- **Sunday**: Race

### Sprint Weekend Structure (select races)

- **Friday**: Free Practice 1 (FP1) and Qualifying
- **Saturday**: Sprint Shootout and Sprint Race
- **Sunday**: Race

## Tire Compounds

Pirelli supplies three dry compounds per race weekend:

- **SOFT (Red)**: Fastest but shortest life
- **MEDIUM (Yellow)**: Balanced performance and durability
- **HARD (White)**: Slowest but longest life

Wet conditions:

- **INTERMEDIATE (Green)**: Light rain, drying track
- **WET (Blue)**: Heavy rain, standing water

## Flag System

| Flag | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 🟢 Green | Track clear, racing conditions |
| 🟡 Yellow | Danger ahead, no overtaking, slow down |
| 🟡🟡 Double Yellow | Great danger, be prepared to stop |
| 🔴 Red | Session stopped, return to pits |
| 🔵 Blue | Faster car approaching (for lapped cars) |
| ⚫🟠 Black/Orange | Car has mechanical problem, pit immediately |
| 🏁 Checkered | Session/race finished |

## DRS (Drag Reduction System)

- **Activation Zone**: Marked section where DRS can be used
- **Detection Point**: Where gap to car ahead is measured
- **Requirement**: Must be within 1 second of car ahead at detection point
- **Effect**: Opens rear wing flap, increases top speed by ~10-15 km/h

> **Note**: DRS rules may change significantly in 2026 regulations.

## Safety Car (SC)

- Deployed for on-track incidents requiring track clearance
- All cars must form queue behind safety car
- No overtaking allowed
- Pit stops permitted (strategic opportunity)
- Lapped cars may unlap themselves before restart

## Virtual Safety Car (VSC)

- Used for minor incidents
- All cars must follow delta time (reduced speed)
- No pit stops advantage (time neutralized)
- Racing resumes immediately when VSC ends

## Pit Stop Rules

- Minimum tire change required: At least 2 different compounds in dry race
- No refueling allowed during race
- Pit lane speed limit: Typically 80 km/h (60 km/h in Monaco pit lane)
- Unsafe release penalty: 5 or 10 second time penalty

## Common Penalties

| Infringement | Penalty |
|--------------|---------|
| Track limits (3 violations) | Warning, then 5s penalty |
| Unsafe release | 5s or 10s time penalty |
| Causing collision | 5s or 10s penalty, possible grid drop |
| Speeding in pit lane | 5s time penalty |
| Ignoring blue flags | 5s penalty or drive-through |
