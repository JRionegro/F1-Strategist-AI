# FIA 2024 Sporting Regulations - Key Extracts

> **Note**: This is a summary of key regulations relevant to race strategy.
> For complete regulations, refer to the official FIA documents.

## Tire Regulations

### Article 30 - Tires

- **30.1**: Only tires supplied by the official supplier (Pirelli) may be used
- **30.2**: Maximum allocation per weekend: 13 sets of dry tires
- **30.3**: Mandatory use of at least 2 different dry compounds during race
- **30.4**: Wet and intermediate tires have unlimited allocation

### Tire Allocation Breakdown

| Session | Sets Available |
|---------|---------------|
| FP1 | 2 sets (returned) |
| FP2 | 2 sets (returned) |
| FP3 | 2 sets (returned) |
| Qualifying | 3 sets |
| Race | 2+ sets |

## Pit Stop Regulations

### Article 34 - Pit Stops

- **34.1**: Pit lane speed limit: 80 km/h (60 km/h at Monaco)
- **34.2**: Cars must use pit lane entry and exit
- **34.3**: Unsafe release results in penalty (5s or 10s)
- **34.4**: Pit equipment must be within designated area

## Safety Car Procedures

### Article 55 - Safety Car

- **55.1**: SC deployed for incidents requiring track intervention
- **55.2**: All cars must form line behind SC, no overtaking
- **55.3**: Pit lane remains open during SC
- **55.4**: SC speed: approximately 120-140 km/h
- **55.5**: Lapped cars may unlap themselves when instructed

### Safety Car Restart

- SC extinguishes lights on final lap in pit lane
- Leader controls pace until Safety Car Line
- Overtaking allowed after Safety Car Line

## Virtual Safety Car

### Article 56 - VSC

- **56.1**: VSC used for less serious incidents
- **56.2**: All cars must follow delta time displayed
- **56.3**: Delta target: approximately 40% slower than race pace
- **56.4**: No pit stop advantage (time neutralized in pit lane)

## Red Flag Procedures

### Article 57 - Suspension

- **57.1**: Red flag for serious incidents or unsafe conditions
- **57.2**: All cars return to pit lane
- **57.3**: Work allowed on cars during suspension
- **57.4**: Tire changes permitted under red flag

### Restart Options

- **Standing Start**: If safe, full formation lap
- **Rolling Start**: Behind Safety Car

## DRS Regulations

### Article 33 - DRS

- **33.1**: DRS may only be used in designated zones
- **33.2**: Activation requires: within 1 second of car ahead at detection point
- **33.3**: DRS enabled after 2 laps from race start
- **33.4**: DRS disabled during yellow flags and wet conditions

## Penalties

### Article 54 - Penalties

| Infringement | Standard Penalty |
|--------------|------------------|
| Exceeding track limits (x3) | Warning, then 5s |
| Causing collision | 5s or 10s |
| Unsafe release | 5s (minimum) |
| Pit lane speeding | 5s |
| Ignoring blue flags | 5s or drive-through |
| Jump start | 5s or 10s |

## Weather Procedures

### Article 25 - Weather

- **25.1**: Race Director may delay start for weather
- **25.2**: Minimum 2 hours between any session
- **25.3**: Standing start may convert to rolling in wet
- **25.4**: Red flag if conditions deteriorate significantly
