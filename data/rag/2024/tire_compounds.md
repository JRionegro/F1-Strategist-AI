# Pirelli 2024 Tire Compounds

## Compound Range

Pirelli uses a sequential naming system (C0-C5) where:

- **C0**: Hardest compound (not typically used)
- **C1**: Very hard
- **C2**: Hard
- **C3**: Medium
- **C4**: Soft
- **C5**: Supersoft (softest)

## Compound Characteristics

### C1 (Hardest Used)

- **Color when HARD**: White
- **Optimal Track Temp**: 30-50°C
- **Characteristic**: Very durable, slow warm-up
- **Best for**: High-degradation circuits, hot conditions
- **Typical life**: 40+ laps

### C2

- **Color when HARD/MEDIUM**: White/Yellow
- **Optimal Track Temp**: 25-45°C
- **Characteristic**: Durable with good performance
- **Best for**: Most circuits as hard option
- **Typical life**: 35-45 laps

### C3

- **Color varies**: White/Yellow/Red by allocation
- **Optimal Track Temp**: 20-40°C
- **Characteristic**: Balanced performance/durability
- **Best for**: Versatile all-rounder
- **Typical life**: 25-35 laps

### C4

- **Color when MEDIUM/SOFT**: Yellow/Red
- **Optimal Track Temp**: 15-35°C
- **Characteristic**: Good grip, moderate degradation
- **Best for**: Lower-grip circuits, cooler temps
- **Typical life**: 20-30 laps

### C5 (Softest)

- **Color when SOFT**: Red
- **Optimal Track Temp**: 15-30°C
- **Characteristic**: Maximum grip, high degradation
- **Best for**: Street circuits, qualifying
- **Typical life**: 10-20 laps

## Wet Weather Compounds

### Intermediate (Green)

- **Use case**: Drying track, light rain
- **Dispersion**: Up to 25 liters/second at 300 km/h
- **Crossover**: Track drying with damp patches

### Full Wet (Blue)

- **Use case**: Heavy rain, standing water
- **Dispersion**: Up to 65 liters/second at 300 km/h
- **Minimum use**: Visible standing water on track

## General Performance Deltas

| Step | Approximate Delta |
|------|-------------------|
| 1 compound step (e.g., C3 to C4) | ~0.5s per lap |
| 2 compound steps (e.g., C2 to C4) | ~1.0s per lap |
| New vs 10-lap-old same compound | ~0.3-0.5s |

## Tire Pressure Regulations

- Pirelli sets minimum pressures for each circuit
- Typically: Front 22-24 psi, Rear 20-22 psi
- Pressures checked after pit stops
- Under-pressure results in penalty

## 2024 Construction Updates

- Reinforced sidewall for reduced puncture risk
- Improved thermal management
- Better consistency across temperature ranges
